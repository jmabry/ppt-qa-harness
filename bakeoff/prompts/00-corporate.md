## "United Next" Strategy Update

### Gauge-Up Progress
- 50-seat regional jets removed: 220 retired since 2021 (target: 280 by 2027)
- Average mainline seats/departure: 174 (up from 151 in 2019)
- ASM growth of +20% vs. 2019 with aircraft count roughly flat

### Fleet Orders & Deliveries

| Aircraft | Firm Orders | Delivered to Date | Remaining | First Delivery |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|-----------|---------------|
| 737 MAX 8 | 255 | 142 | 113 | In service |
| 737 MAX 9 | 175 | 88 | 87 | In service |
| 737 MAX 10 | 200 | 0 | 200 | Awaiting FAA cert |
| 787-9 (new) | 100 | 18 | 82 | 2023 |
| A321neo | 120 | 0 | 120 | ~2028 |
| A350-900 | 45 | 0 | 45 | ~2027 |

**Retirements in progress:** 757-200/300 (phase-out through 2028), 767-300ER (replacing with 787/A350), 777-200 (older frames, 2024-2026)

**Boeing delivery risk:** FAA production cap of 38 MAX/month post-Alaska door plug incident (Jan 2024). A321neo order provides strategic hedge.

### Key 2025 Risks
- Boeing MAX delivery pace (FAA production cap)
- Domestic fare environment (industry capacity discipline)
- Macroeconomic / tariff uncertainty impacting consumer demand
- Fuel price volatility
- MAX 10 certification timeline

### Macro Sensitivity
- Every 1% GDP contraction → ~1.5-2% revenue impact
- United's diversification (premium, international, loyalty) positioned as recession hedge
- International revenue (35% of total) partially insulates vs. domestic-heavy carriers
